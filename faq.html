    What is the study about?

    This study is about how COVID-19 spreads to people who live together. It is important for this research to get information from as many people who live together as soon as possible after a person has a positive COVID test. People who just recently received a positive COVID-19 test and the people that they live with are being asked to participate in this research study.

    How long is the study?

    The study will last about 4-6weeks. Completing screening, consent and enrollment into the study will take approximately 30 minutes. Each day you collect a COVID-19 test sample and answer questions on the study website will take 15-20 minutes. On the first day of sample collection, we also will ask you to collect a few drops of blood from your arm using a special device. This should take just about 5 minutes. At the end of 10 days, we will ask you to complete a few questions on the 10th day of the collection period. These questions will take about 10 minutes. Approximately 4 weeks after starting the study, you will be asked to collect a few more drops of blood from your arm using the same special device and complete a final set of questions. This will take 10-15 minutes.

    Who is conducting the study?

    The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention is sponsoring this study. Westat, a research company based in Rockville, Maryland is managing the study and collecting information from you and the people with whom you live.

    How many days after testing positive can I participate in the study?

    We would like you and other people living in your household to agree to be in the study and start study activities as soon as possible after you (or another household contact) tests positive for COVID-19. If several of your household contacts do not agree to be in the study within 1 to 3 days of the test date of the household contact who was COVID-19 positive, your household may not be eligible for the study. The exact timing depends on whether the person who was COVID-19 positive had symptoms and when they began.

    What if a household contact does not wish to participate? If a person in the household does not want to participate in the study, will the whole household not be able to participate?

    The majority of people in the household need to participate for the household to be eligible. If the household is only two people, then both people need to agree to be in the study. If three people are living in the same household, then two of the three household contacts must agree to participate. One of the household participants must be the person who had the recent COVID-19 positive test.

    What will happen in this research study?

    After you complete the screener questions and agree to be in the study, we will reach out to your household contacts to see if they are interested in being in the study too. If the majority of your household agrees to be in the study, everyone will need to complete the enrollment questions. You and the people you live with will be asked questions about yourself, your health and exposure to COVID-19. We will also ask if you have been vaccinated for COVID-19 and ask you to provide details about vaccinations (such as when and where you were vaccinated). These questions will be on the study website.

    You will be asked to collect a COVID-19 test sample every day (by a swab in your nose or saliva sample) for 10 days in a row. You also will be asked to answer a few questions everyday on the study website about how you are feeling, if you took any medications and how much time you were around the people in your household. On the first day, we also will ask you to put a special device on your arm to collect a few of drops of blood for testing. You will need to ship the test kits to a laboratory for testing for COVID-19. It will take 15-20 minutes to complete the test and answer the questions every day. About 4 weeks later, we will ask you to use the special device on your arm again to collect a few drops of blood for testing and mail in the sample. We will also ask a few questions on the study website about the people you live with and if you saw a doctor or other healthcare worker during the study. We also ask permission for the study to keep these samples for future research.

    Does my child or all my children have to be in the study?

    We would like all people who live together to be in the study, including children of all ages, but it is not required that every person in the household participates. We want to have at least 2/3 of household contacts to participate in the study if they are eligible.

    To thank you for the time involved in participating in the study, you will receive a gift card for activities you complete. You can receive up to $100 depending on what study activities you complete. Reimbursement will be given to each person, including children, for completing groups of activities as shown below:

    • $50 after answering questions for study enrollment and collecting 7 days of COVID-19 test samples, completing daily questions on the study website for 7 days and completing the first blood drop collection

      • OR

    • $75 after answering questions for study enrollment and collecting 10 days of COVID-19 test samples, completing daily questions on the study website for 10 days and completing the first blood drop collection

    If you answer questions for study enrollment and collect 7 or 10 days of COVID-19 test sample collection, we will also reimburse you for the final collection of blood drops at 4 weeks after the start of the study. This includes:

    • $25 for the second set of blood drops and final questions 4 weeks after collecting at least 7 days of COVID-19 test samples.
    Do I have to record my information within a certain timeframe?

    Please complete the study questions, COVID-19 test samples and blood as noted in the instructions.

    How long will I need to collect the COVID-19 sample collection?

    It should take you just a few seconds to complete the COVID-19 test. It will take a few more minutes to answer about 12 questions on the study website about the COVID-19 test collection and report any symptoms you experienced in the day before you collected the COVID-19 test sample and how you interacted with other people in your home and outside your home in the past day.

    What if I made an error while doing my COVID-19 test or collecting the blood drops? What should I do?

    Please contact the study using the study website help function www.TrackCOVIDatHome.org, the study email at COVIDatHome@westat.com or toll-free number 1-855-981-0720. We will let you know what to do and decide how best to resolve this issue.

    What if I missed a COVID-19 test collection one day?

    If you missed one of the COVID-19 tests, just be sure to complete your test the next day and answer the daily diary questions. It is important to collect at least 7 tests during the 10-day testing period.

    The Point of Contact for your household will receive reminders to collect the COVID-19 test sample every day. This adult is responsible for reminding all household members to complete the COVID-19 test and the daily diary questions. However, each person age 13 and over with individual contact information will receive reminders by text and/or email and may be contacted directly by phone by the evening if they have not completed their COVID-19 test or their daily diary questions on the study website.

    What if I missed a day of answering the daily diary questions?

    The Point of Contact for your household will receive reminders to collect the COVID-19 test sample. They are responsible for reminding all household contact every day to complete the COVID-19 test and complete the daily diary questions on the study website.

    If you are late completing the daily diary questions, please complete them as soon as possible if the questions for that day are still open to you on the study website. The daily diary questions for one day will not be available for you to answer the next day. We want to match the answers to the daily diary questions to the COVID-19 test that you do each day.     If I am doing the COVID-19 test, do I need to collect blood drops from my arm as well? Why are you requesting blood drops?

    Yes. Please also use the special device to collect a few drops of blood in the study supplies. The blood drops will be tested to see if you ever had COVID-19.

    How will by my health information be safeguarded?

    Your COVID-19 samples will be tested for COVID-19 at a certified testing laboratory. If your COVID-19 test sample shows a positive COVID-19 test result, your personal identifiable information such as your name, telephone number, age, and gender will be shared with the local health department. Testing laboratories are required by law to report positive COVID-19 test results to the local health department so that they can identify, monitor, assess or investigate the COVID-19 outbreak.

    Other than required reporting of positive COVID-19 results, we will share information that you give us for the study only with other study researchers, and those records will not contain your name or other information that could identify you. However, if you give the Point of Contact for the household permission to access your study information, they will see all your answers.

    This research study has a Certificate of Confidentiality from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). Unless you say it is okay, study staff cannot release information that may identify you for a legal action, a lawsuit, or as evidence. This protection applies to requests from federal, state, or local civil, criminal, administrative, legislative, or other proceedings. As an example, the Certificate would protect your information from a court subpoena.

    There are some important things that you need to know. The Certificate DOES NOT protect your information if a federal, state, or local law says it must be reported. For example, some laws require reporting of abuse, communicable diseases, and threats of harm to yourself or others. The Certificate CANNOT BE USED to stop a federal or state government agency from checking records or evaluating programs. The Certificate DOES NOT stop reporting required by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The Certificate also DOES NOT stop your information from being used for other studies if allowed by federal regulations.

    Study staff may release your information when you say it is okay. For example, you may give them permission to release information to insurers, your doctors, or any other person not connected with the study. The Certificate of Confidentiality does not stop you from releasing your own information. It also does not stop you from getting copies of your own information.

    What is a household?

    A household is a shared living space between two or more people. For this study, correctional facilities, long-term care facilities, boarding schools, hostels, dormitories, or other similar institutionalized/congregate settings are not considered households.

    How will I send the COVID-19 test sample and blood drops from my arm back to the study?

    You will receive a box with a FedEx shipping label. The study instructions will tell you how to pack the COVID-19 test sample and blood kits into the box and send it back to the testing laboratory.

    Is there any cost to me for participating in the study?

    It does not cost anything to take part in this research study.

    How do I learn more about the study?

    You may learn more information by visiting the study website at www.TrackCOVIDatHome.org. The study is sponsored by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and you can find more information about this study on the CDC website (CDCURL).

    Will my information be used for any other studies?

    When you agree to be in the study, you will also be asked to agree or not for your COVID-19 test sample and blood drops to be stored for future research studies. If you agree, you will not be notified if your samples are used for future research. However, your name or address will not be shared. The researchers will only see your ID number.

    Can I withdraw from the study at any time?

    Yes. This study is voluntary and you can stop being in the study at any time. We hope you will not but it is up to you.

    How did you get my information?

    If you tested positive for a COVID-19 test you took at a national laboratory, you received an invitation to this study when you saw your COVID-19 test results online in your account with the national laboratory.

    If you lived with someone who tested positive for COVID-19 from a test at a national laboratory, this person was asked for the names and email addresses of the people they live with and you were sent an email about the study to see if you wanted to participate.

    What is the age limit for participants?

    There is no age limit for this study. All household members are asked to participate in the study.

    Will I be contacted with my test results?

    The COVID-19 test results will be available on the study website. You will need to log in to see the results. If you are age 13 or over and gave permission for the Point of Contact (who is an adult living in the household) that person also will be able to see the test results. Adults and children ages 13 to 17 will be asked if other household members have permission to the results.

    Are there any other organizations involve in the study?

    The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is sponsoring the study. Two testing laboratories, Quest Diagnostics Inc. and Vault COVID-19 test kits may be used to collect samples to test for COVID-19.
