Consent Form

This consent form will give you information about the study and your rights as a research participant. Being in this study is voluntary. Please read this consent form carefully.

What is this study about?

Thank you for your interest in the [National Respiratory Virus Transmission Network Study]! This study is being done so that researchers can learn about how viruses, like COVID-19, are passed among people who live together. It is important for this research to get information from as many people who live together as soon as possible after a person has a positive COVID test. People who just recently received a positive COVID-19 test and the people that they live with, are being asked to participate in this research study. The study will last about six weeks.

Westat, a research company based in Rockville, Maryland is managing the study and collecting information from you and the people you live with. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is sponsoring the study.

What will happen in this research study?

We are asking you if you can take part in this study. We are asking you and the people who live with you to swab your nose and answer questions on the study website about how you are feeling for 10 days. We also will ask you to prick your finger to put a drop of blood on a card. The nasal swab is like a long Q-tip that you put inside your nose. You will need to ship the swabs to a laboratory for testing. We will ask you to answer questions about your health and recent activities. We will also ask if you have been vaccinated for COVID-19 and to provide details about vaccinations (such as when and where you were vaccinated). About 3 to 4 weeks later, we will ask you to prick your finger again and put a drop of blood on a card and then mail the blood collection card to a lab for testing. We also ask permission for the study to keep these samples for future research.

One person in your house should be the point of contact (POC).

Do I have to be in the study?

Your participation is voluntary. You may choose not to answer any questions on the study website, stop swabbing your nose at any time, or not put a drop of blood on the card.

What are the benefits of being in this research study?

By choosing to participate in this study, you will be helping researchers learn more about how respiratory viruses spread among people who live together. If you are living with someone who tested positive for COVID-19, you might benefit from finding out whether or not you also got COVID-19.

Are there risks for being in this research study?

You may experience some physical discomfort when you collect your nasal swabs or the blood spot. You may feel anxious or worried about taking a COVID-19 test. There is a very small risk that your personal information or data could be revealed. Other people in your household may be able to know if you have COVID-19 or not. However, the research team makes every effort to protect your privacy.

What about confidentiality?

We will only share information that you provide as part of the study with other study researchers, and those records will not contain your name or other information that could identify you.

However, if your nasal swabs shows a positive COVID-19 test result, your personal identifiable information such as your name, telephone number, age, and gender will be shared with the local health department. Testing laboratories are required by law to report positive COVID-19 test results to the local health department so that they can identify, monitor, assess or investigate the COVID-19 outbreak.

Is there any cost me?

It does not cost anything to take part in this research study.

Will I be paid take part in this research study?

To thank you for the time involved in participating in the study, you will receive a gift card for up to $100 depending on what study activities you complete.
